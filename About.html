About Me

Not much really to tell about me. My real name is Colby AKA Scolpin. I'm a web designer whose still in school majoring in web design and management. And since I'm still learning, that is why nothing on this page is very fancy.

What's "The United Personalities"

I knew you were going to ask that! The United Personalities comes from an idea that my friends came up with back in high school. In high school, I got along with girls a lot better than I did with guys. Guys were always talking about sports and other stuff that I had no knowledge of and no interest in. Girls, on the other hand, were easier to talk to, cuter, and a lot more interesting. So, because of this I had a lot of friends that were girls (Clarification! I did not have lots of girlfriends. I was just hung around a lot of girls). One day, the girls were talking about their boyfriends and one of them said "You know what? Guys suck!" I replied of course: "Hey! What about me?" The reply I received surprised me: "Oh Colby, you don't count. You think more like a girl than a guy. It's like your subconscious is a girl." And with that, my alter ego was born: "Lola" So then I had two personalities; myself and Lola, my subconscious.

From there, the joke just continued and we just started to name my other "personalities." Here's the break down:

  • Colby: Me, myself, and I
  • Lola: My subconscious. generally considered to be evil in most cases
  • The Fox: Foxes are my favorite animal and it's been said that I am cunning and clever, like a fox
  • Scolpin: My online personality. No one can deny that we act differently online than how we act in real life
  • The Fifth: The personality you would see if I ever got angry, which doesn't happen very often. Consider The Fifth to be kinda like the hulk

And so, that's basically where the name "The United Personalities" came from. Its me and my "personalities" working together on a set goal. Please note: the name does not imply that I have Multiple Personality Disorder. The name of the site just came from an inside joke in high school.
